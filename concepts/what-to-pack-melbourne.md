# What to Pack for Melbourne (July)

Melbourne in July is mid-winter — cold, wet, and Variable. "Four seasons in one day" is real.

## Temperature Range
- Daytime: 6–14°C (43–57°F)
- Nights: 4–8°C (39–46°F)
- Feels colder with wind and rain

## Essentials

### Clothing
- **Layers** — merino base, fleece/wool mid, waterproof shell outer. Do NOT bring single thick layers.
- **Warm jacket** — packable down or insulated rain jacket
- **Beanie + gloves** — often needed outdoors, especially at penguin parade
- **Scarf** — useful for wind and sudden cold
- **Comfortable walking shoes** — waterproof preferred; Melbourne's laneways and day trips involve lots of walking
- **Umbrella** — compact travel umbrella essential; Melbourne's rain is sudden and random
- **Jeans/trousers** — avoid shorts; legs get cold fast in July

### Electronics
- **Phone + power bank** — essential for transport apps (PTV), maps, tour confirmations
- **AU/NZ power adapter** — Type I plug (if coming from Taiwan)
- **Camera** — great for Great Ocean Road, Puffing Billy, coastal walks

### Toiletries & Health
- **Moisturizer** — winter air is dry
- **Lip balm** — same reason
- **Hand sanitizer** — useful after market visits
- **Any regular medications** — chemists are plentiful but brand names differ
- **Cold/flu tablets** — short-stay pharmacies available

### Trip Documents
- **Passport** (for international entry)
- **Flight + hotel confirmations** (printed or offline)
- **Tour vouchers** (KKday/Klook — screenshot on phone)
- **Travel insurance** — strongly recommended for July

## What NOT to Bring
- Heavy formal clothing — the vibe is casual
- Flip-flops or open sandals — totally impractical in July
- Sunscreen — unlikely to need it in July (UV is low in winter)

Melbourne accommodation is mostly heated, so you won't need extreme indoor clothing. The key is layers that you can add/remove as the temperature swings 8°C in a single afternoon.
