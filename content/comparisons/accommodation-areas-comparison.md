# Accommodation Areas Comparison

Melbourne has three main accommodation zones for a trip like this. Each has a distinct character, price level, and practical trade-offs.

## Quick Comparison

| Dimension | Melbourne CBD | Fitzroy | St Kilda |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Price (mid-range hotel)** | ~NT$2,695–NT$4,492/night | ~NT$2,246–NT$4,042/night | ~NT$2,021–NT$3,593/night |
| **Transport to attractions** | Central hub, easy Myki | Tram to CBD, close to many attractions | Tram 96 to CBD, far from day trip departure points |
| **Character** | Business, convenience | Alternative, cafes, pubs, arts | Beach, relaxed, bakeries |
| **Nightlife** | Bars and restaurants | Pubs, live music, local scene | Beach bars, Acland Street |
| **Best for** | First-time visitors, transport convenience | Culture-focused, foodies, long stays | Relaxed beach vibe, early starts to Mornington |
| **Walkability** | Excellent (everything in grid) | Good (walkable streets, near CBD) | Limited (need tram for most things) |

## Melbourne CBD

**Pros:**
- Everything is reachable by tram, train, or foot
- Free Tram Zone covers most of the CBD — no Myki cost for inner travel
- Close to Flinders Street Station for day trips (departures from Southern Cross are more common)
- Wide range of restaurants and bars

**Cons:**
- Can feel generic / business-oriented
- No neighborhood character
- Can be noisy (weekend nightlife)

**Best picks:** Flinders Lane, Little Collins Street, Bourke Street (all in Free Tram Zone)

## Fitzroy

**Pros:**
- Melbourne's most interesting neighborhood — street art, vintage shops, alternative cafes
- Walking distance to CBD (across the Yarra or via tram)
- Excellent pub and live music scene
- Generally cheaper than equivalent CBD hotels

**Cons:**
- Less convenient for day trip transport (often need to tram to Southern Cross first)
- Smaller range of hotels; more Airbnb/hostel private rooms
- No beach access

**Best picks:** Gertrude Street, Brunswick Street area, Smith Street (gentrifying end)

## St Kilda

**Pros:**
- Only beach-adjacent option of the three
- Iconic: St Kilda Pier, Luna Park, Acland Street bakeries
- Tram 96 is scenic and direct to CBD
- Best accommodation for Mornington Peninsula day (closest departure point for Peninsula routes)

**Cons:**
- Farthest from most day trip departure points (Puffing Billy, Great Ocean Road tours)
- Limited dining variety compared to CBD/Fitzroy
- Can feel isolated if you want to explore more than beach/pier

**Best picks:** Acland Street area (walk to everything), The Esplanade Hotel area

## Verdict

**For most travellers:** CBD or Fitzroy. Both are excellent bases. CBD wins on transport convenience; Fitzroy wins on character and food.

**For Mornington Peninsula focus (Days 7–8):** St Kilda makes sense as the last 2 nights before departure — closest to the Peninsula and more relaxed before flying out.

**Avoid:** Southbank (unless you specifically want arts precinct proximity). It's pleasant but less convenient than CBD or Fitzroy for getting around.
