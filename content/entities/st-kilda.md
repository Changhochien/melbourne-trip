# St Kilda

Melbourne's most famous seaside suburb — a beachside community with a grand pier, a failed fun park history, and a persistent, slightly faded glamour. Easy reach from the CBD by tram 96.

## Key Facts
- **Tram:** Route 96 — Sydney Road to St Kilda (World's most tram line)
- **Distance from CBD:** ~6km south

## Key Landmarks

### St Kilda Beach
Wide, safe, popular on hot days. Grey sand, cold water (Southern Ocean). Not a swimming beach for most — too rough.

### St Kilda Pier
The 1880s pier — historic Kelpie kiosk at the end, penguin colony below the breakwater.

### Luna Park
The vintage fun park that somehow survives — the entrance is the mouth of a giant cartoon face. Operating since 1912. Tickets are separate for each ride.

### Acland Street
The eat street — Jewish bakeries (the famous Dagmar's), St Kilda Cake House, restaurants. Slightly touristy but solid.

### Luna Park and the Esplanade Hotel
The "Espy" — legendary punk/rock venue with multiple bars, live music, and history.

## What It's Known For
- Beach culture and pier walks at sunset
- Live music history (the Espy, the Palais)
- Bakeries and Acland Street food
- The St Kilda Festival (summer, free)
