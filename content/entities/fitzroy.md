# Fitzroy

Melbourne's oldest inner suburb and its most alternative. Just north of the CBD across the Yarra, Fitzroy is a grid of Victorian terraces, street art, op shops, and some of the best pubs and restaurants in the city. The gentrification tension is real and visible.

## Key Streets

### Brunswick Street
The main strip — pubs, cafes, live music venues, vintage shops. Gets busy on weekends.

### Johnston Street
Spanish and Italian restaurants and bars. Fitzroy's Latin Quarter.

### Gertrude Street
More local, less touristy — interesting boutiques and neighborhood spots.

### Smith Street
Was rough, now rapidly gentrifying — new restaurants, galleries, affordable end of the market.

## What It's Known For
- Alternative culture and history (Melbourne's first suburb to see gay liberation)
- Live music (the Tote, the Old Bar — famous as the venue where The Vines were discovered)
- Op shops and vintage
- Pubs with character (The Napier, The Union Club)
