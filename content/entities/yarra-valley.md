# Yarra Valley

Victoria's premier wine region, northeast of Melbourne. Cold-climate wines — primarily pinot noir and chardonnay — plus farmland, produce, and Australian wildlife. One hour from the Melbourne CBD.

## Key Towns

### Healesville
The main hub of the Yarra Valley. Small-town main street with cafes and pubs. Close to Healesville Sanctuary and Yarra Valley dairy farms. Good base for exploring.

### Yarra Glen
Smaller, more rural. Cellar doors scattered along the valley floor and hillside.

### Coldstream
Location of Yering Station — one of the region's oldest and most famous wineries.

## Key Activities

### Healesville Sanctuary
Australia's native wildlife, open-range bush setting. Koalas, kangaroos, wombats, platypus. One of the best wildlife experiences outside a zoo. Tickets must be booked online.

### Yarra Valley Wineries
Cool-climate wines. Pinot noir is the standout — some of the best in Australia. Chardonnay is excellent. Notable producers:
- Yering Station
- De Bortoli (famous for Noble One botrytis semillon)
- Oakridge
- Coldstream Hills
- Innocent Bystander

### Yarra Valley Chocolaterie
On the drives between wineries — surprisingly good free samples.

### Hot Air Ballooning
Popular Yarra Valley activity — sunrise flights, champagne breakfast after. Booked through operators like Go Wild Balloons or Balloon Sunrise. Weather dependent.

## Getting There
- **From Melbourne CBD:** ~1 hour, M1 north then Melba Highway
- **Car essential** — cellar doors are spread out, no public transport connects them
- Can do as a guided tour from Melbourne if no car
