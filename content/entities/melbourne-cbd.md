# Melbourne CBD

The Central Business District — the historic heart of Melbourne, bounded by Flinders Street, Spring Street, Bourke Street, and Spencer Street. Australia's largest city centre by commercial throughput.

## Overview
Melbourne's CBD is a dense grid of heritage arcades, towering skyscrapers, and hidden laneways. Despite being one of the most walkable city centres in the world, most visitors barely scratch the surface. The real Melbourne lives in the laneways branching off Collins and Little Collins Streets.

## Key Precincts

### Collins Street
High-end retail, historic banking precinct, European feel.

### Little Collins Street
Cafes, boutiques, lesser-known laneway entrances.

### Flinders Lane
Galleries, design studios, local fashion.

### Bourke Street Mall
Shopping hub, tram interchange, Degavesque.

### Elizabeth Street
Slope running north-south, Queen Victoria Market at its northern end.

## Getting Around
Tram routes 19, 57, 59 run through the CBD. The Free Tram Zone covers everything inside the "magic rectangle" — no Myki needed.
