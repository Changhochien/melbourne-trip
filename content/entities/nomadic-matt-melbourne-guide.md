# Nomadic Matt Melbourne Guide

Budget travel guide perspective on Melbourne from Nomadic Matt (updated 2026).

## Core Advice from the Guide

- Melbourne is a walking city — most sights in the CBD are walkable
- The myki card is essential for trams, trains, and buses
- Free things to do: street art in laneways, Federation Square, Queen Victoria Market, St Kilda beach
- Great Ocean Road is the most popular day trip — allow a full day
- Melbourne's coffee culture is world-class — even budget travelers can find excellent cheap coffee
- Yarra Valley is accessible but wine tours can be pricey — consider self-drive for budget control

## Tips

- **Accommodation:** Hostels in the CBD are ~~NT$673–NT$1,123/night; Airbnb in Fitzroy/St Kilda often cheaper for longer stays
- **Food:** Market Lane Coffee for cheap good coffee; Queen Victoria Market for self-catering
- **Transport:** Myki explorer pass for tourists; daily cap is ~~NT$224/day for inner suburbs
- **Timing:** March–May and September–November are shoulder season with lower prices
