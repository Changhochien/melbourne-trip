# Nomadic Matt — Melbourne Travel Guide (Updated 2026)

Source: https://www.nomadicmatt.com/travel-guides/australia-travel-tips/melbourne/
Author: Nomadic Matt (Matthew Karsten)
Retrieved: April 2025

## Overview
Melbourne is described as Matt's favourite city in Australia. Key qualities:
- Incredible food scene
- Cafe culture
- Art scene
- Laid-back vibe
- Great parks
- Excellent base for day trips

## Top 5 Things to See and Do

### 1. Admire the 12 Apostles (Great Ocean Road)
- Most iconic sight in Australia
- Part of the Great Ocean Road (UNESCO National Heritage-listed)
- 275km from Melbourne city
- Best seen on a multi-day drive or full-day tour
- Full-day tour available via KKday/Klook

### 2. Fitzroy Gardens
- Historic Victorian-era garden, created 1848
- 26 hectares (64 acres)
- Free guided walking tours on Saturdays at 10am
- Near Melbourne CBD

### 3. Royal Botanic Gardens
- 34 hectares (86 acres)
- Over 50,000 plants
- Free entry
- Guided walks available (Aboriginal Heritage Walk, Forest Therapy, Secret Life of Birds)
- Cafe: The Terrace

### 4. Sunset at St Kilda Beach
- Faces due west — stellar sunsets
- Historic pier from 1800s
- Small penguin colony at the breakwater (do not feed or touch)
- Nightlife, restaurants, and bars nearby

### 5. Queen Victoria Market
- Open-air market, opened 1878
- Mix of food sellers and knick-knack vendors
- Weekend outdoor vending bigger than weekday
- Over 10 million visitors per year
- Free wine samples at Swords Wines in the food hall

## Other Notable Activities

### Cafe Culture
- Café tours available: Café Culture Walk, Melbourne Coffee Lovers Walking Tour
- Notable cafes: Terror Twilight, Auction Rooms, Little Rogue
- Key areas: Fitzroy, CBD laneways

### Wine Tours
- Mornington Peninsula: 45 min from city, 50+ wineries
- Yarra Valley: most wine tour destinations
- Full-day wine tours from Melbourne: ~155 AUD/person

### St Kilda Nightlife
- Inexpensive restaurants, bars, clubs
- The Espy (Esplanade Hotel) — live music venue

## Typical Costs (Matt's Estimates)
Note: These are Matt's estimates; Numbeo data from April 2026 is more current.

### Daily Budget Breakdown
- Hostel dorm: ~25-35 AUD/night
- Budget hotel: ~100-130 AUD/night
- Food: ~20-40 AUD/day (cook vs eat out)
- Transport: ~10-20 AUD/day
- Activities: ~20-50 AUD/day

### Suggested Daily Budgets
- Budget: 60-80 AUD/day (~1,350-1,800 TWD)
- Mid-range: 120-180 AUD/day (~2,700-4,000 TWD)
- Splurge: 250+ AUD/day (~5,600+ TWD)

## How to Get Around
- Trams: Extensive network, Free Tram Zone in CBD
- Myki card: Required for trains/buses outside free zone
- Bicycle: Hire bikes to explore
- Walking: CBD and inner suburbs very walkable

## When to Go
- Best: March-May (autumn) or September-November (spring)
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Festivals, but hot and crowded
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Cold but cozy, fewer tourists, cheaper accommodation

## Key Tips
- Melbourne has excellent coffee culture — don't miss the cafes
- Book day tours in advance, especially Great Ocean Road
- Use the free City Circle tram to see major sights
- Explore laneways — they're the heart of Melbourne's culture
- Check out local markets for cheap, excellent food
- Consider a Myki for regional train travel to day trip destinations
