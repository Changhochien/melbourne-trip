# Fitzroy Research

## What It Is
Melbourne's oldest inner suburb (established 1839), immediately north of the CBD across the Yarra River. Known for Victorian terrace architecture, alternative culture, street art, live music venues, pubs, op shops, and a thriving cafe/restaurant scene. Gentrification is visible and ongoing — working-class history colliding with hipster present.

## Key Streets

### Brunswick Street
The main strip. Pubs (The Old Bar, The Napier), live music venues (The Tote — famous as the venue where The Vines were discovered), cafes, vintage clothing shops. Gets busy Thursday–Sunday evenings.

### Johnston Street
Spanish and Italian restaurants and bars — Fitzroy's Latin Quarter. Good-value tapas and wine. Quieter during the day.

### Gertrude Street
More local, less touristy — interesting boutiques, neighbourhood pubs, and shops without the Brunswick Street crowds.

### Smith Street
Was rough and working-class; now rapidly gentrifying. New restaurants, galleries, and a good cheap eats end of the market. The Collingwood side (Smith Street crosses into Collingwood) has even more new openings.

## What It's Known For
- Alternative and LGBTQ+ history (Melbourne's first suburb for gay liberation)
- Live music (The Tote, The Old Bar, The Union Club)
- Op shops and vintage clothing
- Hole-in-the-wall cafes (among Melbourne's best)
- Street art (laneways off Brunswick and Johnston)
- The Napier Hotel, Cookie, and other iconic pubs

## Getting There
- Tram 96 from Bourke Street (CBD) to Brunswick Street
- Tram 12 from Collins Street to Gertrude Street
- 20-minute walk from Flinders Street Station via Princes Bridge

## July Specifics
Fitzroy is excellent in winter — all the pubs, live music venues, and cafes are warm and lively. The street art is visible year-round. Fewer tourists than summer. A Fitzroy pub with a fireplace and a good wine list is an ideal cold Melbourne evening.

## Nearby
- Collingwood (adjacent, similar character)
- Melbourne CBD (across the Yarra, easily reachable)
- North Melbourne (north of Fitzroy, more working class)
