# Wikivoyage — Great Ocean Road

Source: https://en.wikivoyage.org/wiki/Great_Ocean_Road
Retrieved: April 2025
License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike

## Overview
The Great Ocean Road is one of the world's great coastal drives. Built by 3,000 returned WWI soldiers as a war memorial, construction began 1919, completed 1932. Originally toll road. Now UNESCO-quality scenic route.

## Route Summary
Melbourne → Geelong → Anglesea → Lorne → Apollo Bay → Port Campbell → Warrnambool

## Key Stops

### Melbourne to Geelong
- ~75km, about 1 hour drive
- Geelong: waterfront, Rip Bridge, Cheese World (for lunch)

### Geelong to Anglesea
- ~45km, ~40 minutes
- Small coastal towns

### Anglesea to Lorne
- ~35km, ~40 minutes
- Scenic coastal views
- Lorne: popular beach town, good coffee stops

### Lorne to Apollo Bay
- ~45km, ~1 hour
- Most scenic mountain-forest section
- Apollo Bay: fishing village, good seafood

### Apollo Bay to Port Campbell
- ~95km, ~1.5 hours
- The highlight section — Twelve Apostles, Loch Ard Gorge, London Bridge
- Most popular stops all within 20km of Port Campbell

### Port Campbell to Warrnambool
- ~70km, ~1 hour
- More of the limestone formations (The Arch, London Bridge, The Grotto)

## The Twelve Apostles
- Most iconic sight along the route
- Best at sunset (crowded in summer)
- July = less crowded, dramatic winter seas
- Helicopter rides available (~$150/person)

## Practical Info

### Self-Drive vs Tour
- **Self-drive:** ~275km one way to Twelve Apostles, ~3.5-4 hours driving
- **Guided tour:** ~14 hours from Melbourne, most common option
- Road is winding in sections — fatigue is real on return at night

### When to Go
- Best light: late afternoon for photos
- July: moody, dramatic seas, fewer crowds, short days
- Summer: long days but crowded, hot

### What to Bring
- Warm layers (it gets cold on the coast)
- Rain jacket (changeable weather)
- Camera
- Comfortable walking shoes

### Booking
- Tours book up in July school holidays — book 1-2 weeks ahead
- Self-drive: no booking needed except accommodation
- Helicopter rides: book at the site

### Accommodation Along the Route
- Port Campbell: most popular overnight stop
- Apollo Bay: more affordable, quieter
- Lorne: beach town atmosphere
- Otway National Park: nature escapes

## Note on Itinerary
Many visitors do this as a long day trip from Melbourne (7am-9pm tour), which is rushed. The route deserves 2-3 days to properly explore. For a 10-day Melbourne trip, a guided day tour is the practical choice.
