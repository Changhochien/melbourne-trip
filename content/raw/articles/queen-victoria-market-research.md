# Queen Victoria Market Research

## What It Is
Melbourne's iconic open-air market at the northern edge of the CBD, operating since 1854. One of the largest and oldest markets in the world. Two distinct sections: the open-air sheds (produce, meat, dairy) and the fully enclosed heated main hall (deli, specialty foods, souvenirs).

## Hours
- **Tuesday:** 6 AM – 3 PM
- **Thursday:** 6 AM – 3 PM
- **Friday:** 6 AM – 5 PM (extended hours)
- **Saturday:** 6 AM – 3 PM
- **Sunday:** 9 AM – 4 PM
- **Monday/Wednesday:** Closed

**Friday is the best day** — extended hours (until 5 PM) and good afternoon bargains.

## What to Buy
- Fresh produce (seasonal fruit, vegetables)
- Meat and poultry (high quality, good prices)
- Dairy and eggs
- Artisan bread and pastries
- Local and imported cheeses
- Seafood
- Clothing and souvenirs (halls on upper level)
- Melbourne-themed gifts and crafts

## Practical Info
- **Address:** Elizabeth Street & Victoria Street, Melbourne VIC 3000
- **Closest tram:** Routes 19, 57, 59 on Victoria Street
- **Myki:** Not needed for the market area (it's in the Free Tram Zone)
- **Budget:** Market is cheaper than supermarkets for produce; meals from the food hall are ~$10–15/person
- **Cash:** Many stalls are cash only; there are ATMs on site

## Covered vs Uncovered
- The main Market Hall (built 1929) is fully enclosed and weatherproof — great for July
- The open sheds are covered but not enclosed — fine in light rain, not ideal in heavy rain
- QVM is the best indoor backup on a rainy Melbourne day

## Market Summer/Winter
- July is low season — fewer tourists, easier to get stall attention, fresh winter produce
- Winter produce: root vegetables, brassicas, citrus, mushrooms
- The hot food hall (with dumplings, noodles, pizza) is covered and heated

## Connection to Concepts
- Melbourne's food and drink scene: the market is a central part of Melbourne's food identity
- The covered main hall works as a rainy day option (links to melbourne-indoor-activities)
