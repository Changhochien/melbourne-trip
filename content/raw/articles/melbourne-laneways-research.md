# Melbourne Laneways Research

## What It Is
A network of hidden pedestrian laneways off Melbourne's main streets — once service alleys and loading docks, now home to street art, boutique shops, cafes, and bars. The laneways are Melbourne's most distinctive urban feature and the physical expression of the city's creative culture.

## Key Laneways

### Hosier Lane
Melbourne's most famous street art laneway — opposite Federation Square off Flinders Street. Every surface is covered in layers of graffiti and stencil art, constantly changing. The unofficial open-air gallery of Melbourne's urban art scene.

### AC/DC Lane
Named for the band, adjacent to Flinders Lane — small, intense street art, famous wall with the AC/DC logo.

### Duckboard Place
Off Collins Street — upscale laneway with restaurants and bars, heritage brick, good for dining.

### Centre Place
Off Collins Street — connects to Degraves Street, coffee shops and boutiques, vibrant lunch spot.

### Degraves Street
Running off Flinders Street — one of Melbourne's most photographed laneways, lined with cafes and street art. Morning coffee and lunch favourite.

### Guildford Lane
Off Lonsdale Street — quieter, galleries and boutiques, more local feel.

### Matchbox
Off Flinders Lane — small laneway with excellent coffee and a small gallery.

### The Tan
Not a laneway but a track — the Princes Bridge to the Shrine of Remembrance running through the Botanic Gardens. A good walking route.

## Street Art Context
Melbourne is one of the world's great street art cities. The Victorian attitude toward graffiti is distinct — the city has embraced street art as legitimate urban culture while maintaining zero-tolerance for tagging (unauthorised writing). Hosier Lane is specifically legalised as a street art space. The result: world-class murals and tags coexist, and the quality is consistently high.

## Getting There
The laneways are in the CBD — walkable from Flinders Street Station. The main cluster is between Flinders Street, Collins Street, and Lonsdale Street. Key walking route: Flinders Street → Hosier Lane → AC/DC Lane → Centre Place → Degraves Street → Collins Street.

## July Specifics
Laneways are partially sheltered by the surrounding buildings — walking between cafes, you'll encounter some rain but the laneways themselves are navigable in Melbourne's showery conditions. The street art is visible in any weather. In July, the cafes are warm refuges — duck into any hole-in-the-wall cafe you pass.
