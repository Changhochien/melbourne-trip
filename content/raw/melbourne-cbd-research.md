# Melbourne CBD Research

## What It Is
Melbourne's Central Business District — the historic grid of heritage arcades, hidden laneways, and high-rise towers that forms the core of Australia's most cosmopolitan city. Bounded by Flinders Street (south), Spencer Street (west), La Trobe Street (north), and Spring Street (east).

## Getting Around

### Free Tram Zone
Melbourne's CBD and some inner suburbs are within the Free Tram Zone — no Myki needed for tram travel within this area. The zone covers everything inside the "magic rectangle" roughly bounded by:
- North: La Trobe Street
- East: Spring Street
- South: Flinders Street
- West: Spencer Street (Southern Cross Station)

### Myki Required
- Beyond the Free Tram Zone (even one stop past the boundary)
- All train travel (including to/from Flinders Street Station)
- Tram travel outside the zone

### Key Tram Routes in CBD
- Route 19 — north-south along Elizabeth Street
- Route 57 — north-south along Elizabeth and Bourke Streets
- Route 59 — east-west along Flinders Street and Elizabeth Street
- Route 96 — east-west through Bourke Street (then south to St Kilda)
- Route 86/35 — City Circle tram (free, tourist-oriented, passes major landmarks)

## Key Streets and Areas

### Collins Street
Melbourne's premier shopping street — high-end boutiques, banking district. Heritage buildings mix with modern towers.

### Bourke Street Mall
Pedestrian shopping mall with Myer and emporium. Also the route of the free City Circle tram.

### Elizabeth Street
Running north-south through the CBD — shops, cafes, and the entrance to the Melbourne Central shopping complex. Route 19 runs along it.

### Little Collins Street
A quieter version of Collins — good for independent boutiques, shoe shops.

### Flinders Lane
Not actually a lane — more of a street. Hub of Melbourne's laneway culture. Cafes, galleries, fashion.

## Arcades

### Block Arcade (1891)
Heritage shopping arcade off Collins Street — Tile Gallery, Hopetoun Tea Rooms. One of Melbourne's most beautiful buildings.

### Royal Arcade (1870)
Melbourne's oldest shopping arcade, connecting Collins and Little Collins Streets via two iconic entrance towers (Gaunt's clock and the Mysore Dyana).

Both are fully enclosed and air-conditioned — good for rain.

## Landmarks
- Flinders Street Station (southern edge)
- Melbourne Town Hall (eastern edge)
- State Library Victoria (northern edge)
- Emporium Melbourne (shopping complex)
- Melbourne Central (shopping complex with shot tower)

## Melbourne Town Hall
Beats-clock building on the corner of Swanston and Collins Streets. Not open for tours but visible from outside. Used as a wedding photography backdrop and occasional event venue.

## Why the CBD is Good in July
- Everything is indoor or covered
- Free Tram Zone means easy transport
- Laneway cafes are warm, cosy, excellent
- Arcades and shopping centres are fully enclosed
- The city's Christmas/summer culture is absent — no crowds, easier to navigate
