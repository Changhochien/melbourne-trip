# Melbourne Indoor Activities (Rainy Day Options)

Melbourne has ~10–12 rainy days in July. These activities are equally good (often better) on wet days.

## Museums & Galleries (Free / Low Cost)

- **NGV (National Gallery of Victoria)** — Free general entry. Three floors of Australian and international art. Great for 2–3 hours.
- **ACMI (Australian Centre for the Moving Image)** — Free entry. Film, TV, and digital culture museum in Fed Square.
- **Melbourne Museum** — Natural history, indigenous culture, dinosaurs. Free entry on Sundays (otherwise ~~NT$336).
- **State Library Victoria** — Beautiful historic reading rooms, free to enter.

## Shopping ( Covered)

- **Queen Victoria Market** — Covered main hall. Open Tue/Thu/Fri/Sat/Sun. Rainy Day: the meat/dairy halls are fully enclosed.
- **Block Arcade + Royal Arcade** — Heritage shopping arcades off Collins Street. Fully covered.
- **Emporium Melbourne** — Upscale shopping centre on Lonsdale Street, connected to Melbourne Central.

## Food & Drink Experiences

- **Lunch at a cafe** — Melbourne's cafe culture is a must regardless of weather. Fitzroy and Collingwood have excellent options.
- **Food court / market lunch** — QVM food hall or South Melbourne Market.
- **Hot chocolate at a specialty cafe** — Perfect rainy afternoon activity.

## Entertainment

- **ACMI film screenings** — Regular film program, cheap tickets.
- **Live music venues** — Melbourne has excellent small venues (see [[st-kilda]] and [[fitzroy]] areas).
- **Bowling or arcade** — Palace Cinema in South Yarra, or one of Melbourne's retro arcades.
- **Library visit** — State Library or local branch libraries are beautiful and free.

## All-Weather Versions of Day Trips

- **Heide Museum of Modern Art** — Indoor gallery + sculpture park; canopy covers most outdoor areas.
- **Werribee Open Range Zoo** — Animals are active in winter, and the undercover areas work for rain.
- **Melbourne Aquarium** — Fully enclosed, great for a rainy morning.

## Connection to Entities
- [[fed-square]] — NGV and ACMI are both in/adjacent to Fed Square
- [[queen-victoria-market]] — Covered market halls, reliable rain backup
- [[melbourne-arts-precinct]] — NGV Southbank complex is entirely indoor
- [[st-kilda]] — Many cafes and venues provide shelter from the cold

## Notes
The key insight for July: don't try to "avoid" rainy days — Melbourne's best activities are equally good indoors. A rainy morning at NGV followed by a hot lunch at a laneway cafe is a genuinely excellent Melbourne day.
