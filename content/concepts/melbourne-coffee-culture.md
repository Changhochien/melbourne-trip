# Melbourne Coffee Culture

Melbourne is widely considered the coffee capital of the world. The city's third-wave coffee scene emerged in the early 2000s, and it remains deeply embedded in daily life — not a trend but a way of being.

## Key Concepts

### The Flat White
Melbourne's most famous coffee export. A espresso-based drink with steamed milk, smaller and with more microfoam than a latte. The story of origin is contested — Market Lane Coffee and various CBD cafes all claim it.

### Hole-in-the-Wall Cafes
A Melbourne specialty — tiny espresso bars with no or minimal seating, serving exceptional coffee to a neighborhood clientele. The true test of a barista.

### The Daily Ritual
Coffee is a non-negotiable part of the day for most Melburnians. Walking to your local is a social act. Sitting with a coffee and the newspaper is disappearing.

## What Makes It Different
- Strong emphasis on single-origin beans and pour-over
- Barista as skilled craftsperson, not service worker
- Cafes compete on quality, not marketing
- Inner-suburb cafes often better than CBD ones
