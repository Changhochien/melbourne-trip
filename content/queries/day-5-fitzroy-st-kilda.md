## Morning: Fitzroy

Melbourne's oldest inner suburb. Alternative culture, street art, op shops, live music.

### Brunswick Street (Fitzroy's main strip)

- **Coffee:** Experience [[melbourne-coffee-culture]] — hole-in-the-wall cafes, single origin, flat white is the standard
- **Brunch:** Market Lane Coffee (Bouverie St), Seven Seeds (Berkeley St)
- **Lunch:** Smith Street has newer restaurants, good value — Little Beauty, Saigon Sunny

### Gertrude Street

- Boutiques, vintage, design shops
- Interesting architecture walk

### Smith Street

- Good for lunch, more affordable than Brunswick St
- Hipster shops, record stores

### Op Shops (Fitzroy specialty)

If you're into thrift shopping:
- **Vintage:** Frock This, Huntersville, Salvos on Brunswick St
- **Op shops:** St Vinnie's, Lifeline on Brunswick St

## Afternoon: St Kilda

Take tram 96 from Fitzroy (or from CBD). Best in afternoon/evening.

### St Kilda Pier + Breakwater

- Walk along the breakwater to the end of the pier
- Views back to Melbourne CBD skyline
- Often pelicans and cormorants around the rocks

### Acland Street

- **Dagmar's Deli** — Jewish bakery, incredible cakes and pastries
- **St Kilda Cake House** — Another legendary bakery
- **The Penguin** — Ice cream shop, institution
- Full of restaurants and bars for evening

### Luna Park

- **Photo stop** — The face at the entrance is a Melbourne icon
- Rides optional — you don't need to spend money here to enjoy it
- Best seen from the beach or pier at sunset

## Sunset at St Kilda

July sunset ~5:15 PM. Head to the pier to watch it.
