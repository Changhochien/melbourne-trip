# Day 4 — Jul 4 (Friday): Markets + CBD Laneways

> Rain back-up plan: NGV International (free), ACMI (paid). This is a walking-heavy day — comfy shoes required.

## Queen Victoria Market (QVM)

**Open:** Tue / Thu / Fri / Sat / **Sun** / Mon (closed Wed)  
**Arrive:** 8–9 AM for best selection and lower prices  
**Location:** Corner of Elizabeth St and Victoria St, Melbourne CBD

| What's There | Notes |
|--------------|-------|
| Produce section | Fruit, veg, deli, cheese. Cash preferred at some stalls |
| Meat hall | Butcher shops, great for picnic supplies |
| Street eats | Baked goods, dumplings, coffee |
| IMQ (shed area) | Clothing, souvenirs, arts and crafts |
| Jim's | The legendary Italian greengrocer in the meat hall |

**Budget for QVM:** ~NT$449–NT$898 for breakfast + lunch supplies. Eat cheap, self-cater.

**Parking:** Free before 9 AM at the QVM car park (enter from Peel St).

## Laneways Walk (Melbourne's Signature)

Start at [[flinders-street-station]], walk through the laneways:

| Laneway | What's There |
|---------|-------------|
| **Hosier Lane** | Street art graffiti — Melbourne's most iconic laneway art |
| **Hardware Lane** | Italian restaurants, good for lunch |
| **Degraves Street** | Cafes, coffee, breakfast spots |
| **Flinders Lane** | Boutiques, vintage clothing |
| **AC/DC Lane** | Famous narrow laneway behind Flinders St |
| **Cohen Place** | Near Degraves, small but packed with cafes |

Arcades to see:
- **Block Arcade** — 1892 heritage, mosaic floors, upmarket shops
- **Royal Arcade** — 1870, oldest in Melbourne, Gaunt's clock

## Afternoon: [[fed-square]] + Southbank

- **Federation Square (Fed Square):** Melbourne's civic plaza, across from Flinders St Station
- **NGV International:** 200+ Australian and international artworks, free general entry, open daily
- **ACMI (Australian Centre for the Moving Image):** Film, TV, digital culture, paid entry

**Recommendation:** Do NGV (free) if you haven't done it. ACMI if you want something more interactive.
