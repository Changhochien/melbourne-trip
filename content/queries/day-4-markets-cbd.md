# Day 4 — Jul 4 (Friday): Markets + CBD

> Four major markets are on today. Arrive early, fuel up at Convent Bakery, walk the laneways. Rain back-up: NGV International (free).

## Morning: Convent Bakery Abbotsford Convent

**[Convent Bakery](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Abbotsford+Convent/)** — Arrive around 9 AM. This converted convent complex has excellent breakfast spots. The adjacent Saturday farmers market is on too.

**Address:** 1 St Heliers St, Abbotsford VIC 3067
**Open:** Wednesday–Sunday
**Getting there:** Tram 12 or 109 from CBD (~15 min)

## Queen Victoria Market (QVM)

**Open:** Tue / Thu / Fri / Sat / Sun (closed Mon/Wed)
**Arrive:** 8–9 AM for best selection  
**Location:** Corner of Elizabeth St and Victoria St, Melbourne CBD

| Section | What's There | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Produce | Fruit, veg, deli, cheese | Cash preferred at some stalls |
| Meat Hall | Butcher shops | Great for picnic supplies |
| Street Eats | Baked goods, dumplings, coffee | Market Lane Coffee on Victoria St |
| IMQ Sheds | Clothing, souvenirs, arts | |
| Jim's | Legendary Italian greengrocer | In the meat hall |

**Market Lane Coffee** — Inside QVM, excellent single-origin espresso. Great mid-morning break.

**Budget for QVM:** ~NT$449–NT$898 for breakfast + lunch supplies.

## Afternoon: Laneways Walk

Start at [[flinders-street-station]], walk through Melbourne's iconic laneways:

| Laneway | What's There |
|---------|-------------|
| **Hosier Lane** | Street art graffiti — Melbourne's most iconic laneways art |
| **Degraves Street** | Cafes, coffee, breakfast spots |
| **Flinders Lane** | Boutiques, vintage clothing |
| **AC/DC Lane** | Famous narrow laneway behind Flinders St |
| **Cohen Place** | Near Degraves, small but packed with cafes |

Arcades:
- **Block Arcade** — 1892 heritage, mosaic floors, upmarket shops
- **Royal Arcade** — 1870, oldest in Melbourne, Gaunt's clock

## Other CBD Stops Today

- **State Library Victoria** — Beautiful reading room, free entry. Great if you need a break from walking.
- **Melbourne Central** — Shopping centre with retail, food court.
