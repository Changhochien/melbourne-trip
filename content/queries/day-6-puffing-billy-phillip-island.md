# Day 6 — Jul 6 (Sunday): Puffing Billy + Penguin Parade

> The longest day. Depart ~7:00 AM, return ~9:30–10:00 PM.
> **Non-negotiable:** FULL warm layers for Penguin Parade — 5-8°C, Southern Ocean wind.

## Before Departure: Pick Up LUNA Croissants

**[Lune Croissanterie](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Lune+Croissanterie+Melbourne+CBD+on+Russell/)** — [7-9 Crosquel R, Melbourne CBD](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Lune+Croissanterie+Melbourne+CBD+on+Russell/). Arrive at 6:30–6:45 AM to join the queue before opening at 7:00 AM. This is Melbourne's most famous croissant bakery — the queue moves fast and they sell out.

**Get:** Classic butter croissant, almond croissant. Bring napkins — no seating.

**Hours:** Mon–Fri 07:30–17:00 | Sat–Sun 08:00–17:00
**Why:** Breakfast on the road to Puffing Billy. Don't eat at the station cafe — Lune is in a different league.

## Puffing Billy Railway

Heritage narrow-gauge railway through Dandenong Ranges. The classic experience:
- Train goes through forest, over timber trestle bridges
- Sit on the ledges of the open-air carriages (traditional)
- Views of the Dandenongs are beautiful in winter mist

**Fares (direct):** puffingbillyrailway.org.au — but booking via KKday/Klook keeps it all in one tour.

## Packing for Today

**MORNING (Puffing Billy):**
- Light layers — Dandenong Ranges can be misty but not brutally cold
- Comfortable shoes

**EVENING (Penguin Parade — CRITICAL):**
- **Thermal base layer** — You will be standing 90+ minutes
- **Windproof outer jacket** — Southern Ocean wind is brutal
- **Beanie / warm hat** — Heat escapes from your head
- **Gloves** — Fingers go numb fast at 5-8°C
- **Scarf / neck warmer**
- **Extra socks** — Cold feet ruin everything
- Hand warmers (buy at 7-Eleven if needed)

July penguin parade = brutal cold. If you under-pack, you'll regret it. There's nowhere to warm up once you're in the viewing area.
