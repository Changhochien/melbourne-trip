# Melbourne Transport Myki Research

## What is Myki?
Melbourne's integrated smart card ticketing system for trains, trams, and buses across metropolitan Melbourne and regional Victoria. Replaced the old paper Metcard in 2012.

## How It Works
- **Top up:** Online, at 7-Eleven stores, at station myki machines
- **Autoload:** Set it and forget — automatically tops up when balance drops below a threshold
- **Cost:** Card itself is $6 (refundable); no expiry on balance
- **Minimum fare:** ~$3.50 for a 2-hour zone 1 trip

## Zones
- **Zone 1:** Melbourne CBD and inner suburbs (where most tourists stay and visit)
- **Zone 2:** Outer suburbs — covered by day trips to Yarra Valley, Mornington Peninsula
- **Free Tram Zone:** CBD and some inner suburbs — no Myki needed for tram travel within the zone

### The Free Tram Zone
Covers the CBD roughly bounded by:
- North: La Trobe Street
- East: Spring Street
- South: Flinders Street
- West: Spencer Street / Southern Cross Station

Within this area, tram travel is free — just board, no tap needed.

**Important:** Flinders Street Station itself is OUTSIDE the Free Tram Zone — you need to tap on/off there.

## Getting to Day Trip Departures

### Great Ocean Road Tours
Most depart from centrally located hotels or from Southern Cross Station. Best to take a tram or train to Southern Cross and wait at the pickup point.

### Sovereign Hill (Ballarat)
Train from Southern Cross to Ballarat (~$20, 1.5 hrs) + bus or taxi from Ballarat Station. Guided tours depart from Melbourne and include transport.

### Puffing Billy (Belgrave)
Train from Flinders Street to Belgrave (~$8, 2 hrs via city loop). Or guided tour from Melbourne.

### Mornington Peninsula Hot Springs
No direct train. Best by car or guided tour. Mornington or Rosebud endpoint on Peninsula.

### Yarra Valley
No direct train to cellar doors. Self-drive or guided tour required.

## Myki and July Travel
July is off-peak for tourist transport. Day trip tours that include transport are more cost-effective than DIY for Sovereign Hill and Great Ocean Road when you factor in train fares + tour logistics.
