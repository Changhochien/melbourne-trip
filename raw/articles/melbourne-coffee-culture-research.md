# Melbourne Coffee Culture Research

## What It Is
Melbourne is widely regarded as the coffee capital of the world — or at least of the non-Italian world. The city is where the third-wave coffee movement took root in Australia, characterised by single-origin espresso, pour-over, flat white, and a culture of hole-in-the-wall cafes that prioritised craft over convenience.

## Key Facts
- Melbourne invented the flat white (along with Wellington, NZ) — both cities claim it; most evidence points to Melbourne cafes in the early 1980s
- There are more specialty coffee roasters per capita in Melbourne than almost anywhere outside of Scandinavia
- The city's laneways and small shopfronts became the natural home of small-batch roasters
- "Hole in the wall" cafes — tiny shopfronts, often just a bench and an espresso machine — are a Melbourne signature

## Where to Go

### Fitzroy (Best Area)
- **Proud Mary** — Collingwood/Fitzroy border; one of Melbourne's most famous specialty cafes
- **Seven Seeds** — Carlton; pioneering specialty roaster
- **St Ali** — South Melbourne; well-established roaster and cafe
- **The Fermentalist** — Fitzroy; fermentation-focused menu, excellent coffee

### CBD and Laneways
- **Patricia Coffee Brewers** — Little Min乐, CBD; tiny, no-seat, standing-only espresso bar
- **Market Lane** — multiple locations (Prahran, QVM); excellent roaster
- **Dukes** — Emporium, CBD; single origin espresso

### Gertrude Street, Fitzroy
The Gertrude Street stretch between Johnston and Smith has a cluster of excellent small cafes — walk it on foot and stop where it looks good.

## What to Order
- **Flat white** — Melbourne's signature; smaller and smoother than a latte
- **Single origin espresso** — most specialty places rotate their beans
- **Pour-over / batch brew** — if you want to taste the origin
- **Magic** — a Melbourne specialty: a strong flat white in a small glass; harder to find outside Melbourne

## Price Range
- Standard cafe espresso: ~$4–5
- Specialty pour-over: ~$6–8
- Most places: accept card and cash

## July Specifics
Melbourne's coffee culture is year-round. In July, the best thing to do is pick a hole-in-the-wall Fitzroy cafe, order a flat white, and watch Melbourne happen around you. The cafe culture is specifically designed for the cold — most small cafes are heated, intimate, and comfortable.
