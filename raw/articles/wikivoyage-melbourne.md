# Wikivoyage Melbourne Guide

Source: https://en.wikivoyage.org/wiki/Melbourne
Retrieved: April 2025
License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike

## Melbourne Overview
Melbourne is described as one of the world's great cosmopolitan cities. Founded 1835 after British settlers purchased land from Aboriginal people. Streets carefully laid out in 1837. Named after William Lamb, 2nd Viscount Melbourne.

## Key Distinctions
- More diverse than most Australian cities
- Cafe culture is central to identity
- Extensive tram network (largest in world)
- Free Tram Zone covers CBD
- Strong arts, music, food, and coffee scene

## Getting Around

### Public Transport
- Transport Victoria coordinates trains, trams, buses
- Myki card required for all public transport
- Free Tram Zone: CBD (check current boundaries)
- Trams criss-cross city; trains extend to suburbs

### By Tram
- Route 96: Sydney Road to St Kilda (world's longest tram route)
- Route 86/35: City Circle tram (free, tourist-oriented)
- AllMelbourne CBD is walkable on the grid

## Key Areas

### CBD
- Grid layout makes navigation easy
- Laneways and arcades between major streets
- Free Tram Zone covers most of CBD

### Fitzroy
- Melbourne's bohemian inner-north suburb
- Brunswick Street, Gertrude Street, Smith Street
- Excellent cafes, pubs, vintage shops
- Walking distance from CBD

### St Kilda
- Seaside suburb, ~6km from CBD
- Budget accommodation option
- Tram 96 from CBD
- St Kilda Beach, Luna Park, Acland Street bakeries

### South Melbourne
- Popular mid-range area
- Market and cafe culture

## What to See & Do

### Markets
- **Queen Victoria Market** — largest open-air market in Southern Hemisphere, 1878. Two blocks. Food + merchandise. Most crowded on weekends.

### Gardens
- **Fitzroy Gardens** — 26 hectares, Victorian era, free guided walks Saturday 10am
- **Royal Botanic Gardens** — 34 hectares, 50,000+ plants, free entry

### Laneways
- Degraves Street, The Causeway, and other laneways for cafes
- Flinders Lane for upscale restaurants
- Street art in Hosier Lane

### Food & Coffee
- Laneways have the best hole-in-the-wall cafes
- South Yarra (Chapel Street), Fitzroy (Brunswick Street)
- Melbourne's Vietnamese food is excellent (Sydney Road)

## Eating
- Melbourne is one of the world's best culinary destinations
- Eating out cheaper than Western Europe but more expensive than North America
- Tipping not customary
- Excellent representation of almost every cuisine

## Drinking
- Distinct difference between 'bars' (small) and 'pubs' (larger)
- Pubs in city and inner suburbs serve restaurant-standard food
- Lunch noon-2PM, dinner 6-10PM

## Sleeping
- Budget areas: CBD and St Kilda
- Also popular: Fitzroy, South Melbourne, Windsor
- January-March (Australian Open, Grand Prix): hostels book out — book ahead

## Stay Safe
- Emergency number: 000
- Take care around tram lines (trams need 100m to stop)
- Be careful crossing near tram stops

## Side Trip Destinations from Melbourne
- **Werribee** — historic mansion + open-range zoo
- **Dandenong Ranges** — national park, gardens, Puffing Billy steam railway
- **Yarra Valley** — wineries
- **Mornington Peninsula** — hot springs, beaches, wineries
- **Great Ocean Road** — coastal drive, Twelve Apostles
- **Phillip Island** — penguins, koalas
- **Ballarat / Sovereign Hill** — gold rush history
