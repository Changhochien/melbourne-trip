# Wikivoyage — Mornington Peninsula

Source: https://en.wikivoyage.org/wiki/Mornington_Peninsula
Retrieved: April 2025
License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike

## Overview
Mornington Peninsula is Melbourne's closest coastal escape — ~1 hour south of the city. The peninsula faces two seas: Port Phillip Bay (calm, swimmer-friendly beaches) on the west, and the Southern Ocean (rugged, dramatic) on the south and east.

## Key Areas

### Western Peninsula (Port Phillip Side)
- **Sorrento:** Historic limestone town, swimming beaches, ferry to Queenscliff
- **Portsea:** Luxury end, rocky beaches, Point Nepean National Park
- **Rye:** Holiday town, family beaches, good base

### Southern Peninsula (Ocean Side)
- **Mount Martha:** Upscale, scenic, Mornington Peninsula Hot Springs
- **Safety Beach:** Quieter, good for families
- **Mount Eliza:** Secluded beaches

### Interior
- **Red Hill:** Wine region heart, cellar doors
- **Main Ridge:** More wineries, farms
- **Merricks:** Wine and produce

## Getting There

### By Car (Best)
- M1 south from Melbourne ~1 hour to Rye
- M1 to Mount Martha ~55 minutes
- Road quality: excellent

### By Public Transport
- No direct train — bus required
- Bus from Frankston station (end of train line)
- V/Line coach to Sorrento
- Not convenient without a car

### Organized Tour
- Most visitors take a tour from Melbourne
- Hot springs tours popular
- Wine tours available

## Activities

### Swimming Beaches (Port Phillip Side)
- Sorrento, Portsea, and Rye have safe, calm waters
- Summer season only (December-March) for swimming
- Winter: not warm enough but beach walks are scenic

### Mornington Peninsula Hot Springs
- Geothermal pools on Mount Martha hillside
- Various temperature pools (37-42°C)
- Book online — sells out quickly
- Best experienced in winter (cold air + hot water contrast)

### Wineries
- Cool-climate region: pinot noir, chardonnay, sparkling wine
- Over 50 cellar doors
- Red Hill and Main Ridge are main concentration areas
- Notable: Ten Minutes by Tractor, Port Phillip Estate, Crittenden

### Point Nepean National Park
- Former military fortifications at the peninsula's tip
- Coastal walks, historic Quarantine Station
- Free entry, $5/day parking
- Bikes can be hired

### Peninsula Creamery
- Famous ice cream shop in Flinders
- Worth stopping at

## Cost Guide
- Hot Springs: ~$50-90/person depending on session length
- Wine tasting: ~$10-15/person (often waived with purchase)
- Lunch at winery restaurant: ~$30-50/person
- Self-catering accommodation: ~$150-250/night for 2BR apartment

## Best Time to Visit
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Best swimming, busiest
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Hot springs ideal, fewer crowds
- Shoulder (Mar-May, Sep-Nov): Best for wineries and walking

## Accommodation
- Rye: Most central for hot springs and beaches
- Sorrento: Historic, ferry access, upmarket
- Flinders: Quiet, scenic, good restaurants
- Airbnb apartments in Rye and Sorrento are most popular for groups
