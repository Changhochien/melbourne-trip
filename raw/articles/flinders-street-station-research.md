# Flinders Street Station Research

## What It Is
Melbourne's central railway station, opened 1910. Iconic yellow heritage dome, main entry to Melbourne's suburban rail network (16 platforms). A Melbourne landmark and the city's most recognisable building.

## Key Facts
- **Address:** Flinders Street, Melbourne VIC 3000
- **Platforms:** 16
- **Operator:** Metro Trains Melbourne
- **Lines served:** All suburban lines (Craigieburn, Upfield, Cragieburn, Sandringham, Glen Waverley, Alamein, Lilydale, Belgrave, etc.)
- **Myki:** Required for all travel; Free Tram Zone does NOT cover Flinders Street Station itself
- **Hours:** 24/7 (trains from ~5 AM to midnight)

## How It Connects to Day Trips
- Many day tours (Great Ocean Road, Sovereign Hill) depart from Southern Cross Station, not Flinders Street
- Some tours offer CBD hotel pickup including Flinders Street area
- Flinders Street is the hub for getting TO tours at Southern Cross (1 tram stop or 10 min walk)

## Nearby
- Fed Square (directly opposite)
- Melbourne Town Hall (adjacent)
- Flinders Lane (laneway entrance)
- Melbourne Arts Precinct (10-min walk south on St Kilda Road)

## Note on Architecture
The station's Beaux-Arts style and distinctive dome have made it a Melbourne symbol. The building is heritage-listed.
