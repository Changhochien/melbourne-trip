# Melbourne Weather July Research

## July in Melbourne — Key Facts

Melbourne is in the Southern Hemisphere, so July is mid-winter. This is the coldest and one of the wettest months.

### Temperature
- **Daytime high:** 6–14°C (43–57°F)
- **Night minimum:** 4–8°C (39–46°F)
- **Average:** ~10°C (50°F) daytime

### Rain
- **Rain days per month:** ~10–12
- **Monthly rainfall:** ~50mm
- Rain is typically showery, not prolonged — Melbourne's "four seasons in one day" pattern
- Pack a compact umbrella regardless of forecast

### Sun and Daylight
- **Sunrise:** ~7:15–7:30 AM
- **Sunset:** ~5:15–5:30 PM
- Only ~10 hours of daylight — days feel short
- UV is low in winter — sunscreen not essential but not harmful to pack

### Wind
- Melbourne is exposed to cold southerly winds from the Southern Ocean
- Wind chill makes 10°C feel like 4–5°C
- Windproof layer is more useful than extra insulation

## Why July is Actually a Good Time to Visit

1. **Accommodation is cheaper** — lowest season; can get 30–40% off peak prices
2. **Fewer tourists** — attractions like Sovereign Hill and Great Ocean Road are less crowded
3. **Daylight is short** — forces realistic itinerary planning; no 14-hour sightseeing days
4. **It's not that cold indoors** — Melbourne buildings are well-heated; cafes and restaurants are warm and comfortable
5. **The contrast with Mornington Hot Springs is perfect** — cold air + hot springs is the ideal winter experience
6. **Penguin parade timing is better** — sunset at 5:15 PM means earlier start and less waiting in cold

## What This Means for Packing

See [[what-to-pack-melbourne]] — the key is layers that can be added/removed throughout the day. Single thick items are worse than multiple thin layers.

## What This Means for the Itinerary

- [[melbourne-indoor-activities]] are essential backup plans for rainy days
- Great Ocean Road tours can be moody and dramatic in winter rain — still worth it
- Puffing Billy is an open-air train — dress warmly but it runs in all weather
- Phillip Island penguin parade is COLD in July — full winter layers essential
- Mornington Hot Springs is ideal in July — contrast between cold air and hot pools is perfect
