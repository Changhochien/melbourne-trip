# Melbourne Arts Precinct Research

## What It Is
Melbourne's Southbank arts district — the concentration of major cultural institutions on St Kilda Road and Flinders Street south of the Yarra. One of Australia's densest arts zones. Free entry to most institutions.

## What's There

### NGV (National Gallery of Victoria)
Australia's oldest and most visited art museum. Two buildings:
- **NGV International** (Flinders Street side) — international collection: Renaissance, Dutch Masters, Impressionism, contemporary international
- **NGV Australia** (Federation Square side) — Australian art: colonial to contemporary, Indigenous Australian art

**Cost:** Free general entry; some special exhibitions require tickets
**Hours:** 10 AM – 5 PM daily
**NGV Friday Nights:** Seasonal evening openings with DJs, bars, and food (October–March typically)

### Arts Centre Melbourne
Performing arts complex — theatre, ballet, opera, music. The gold-tiled spire is a Melbourne landmark. Hosts major performances year-round.

### ACMI (Australian Centre for the Moving Image)
Film, TV, and digital culture museum. Screens classic films, hosts exhibitions on game design, animation, and interactive media. Free entry.

**Hours:** 10 AM – 5 PM daily
**Cost:** Free (some special exhibitions may charge)

### Melbourne Arts Precinct Tours
Free guided tours of the NGV run daily at 11 AM and 2 PM (meet at the information desk).

## Getting There
- From Flinders Street Station: 10-minute walk south on St Kilda Road
- Tram: Route 1, 3, 5, 6, 8, 16, 64, 67 on St Kilda Road
- From CBD: Easy tram or walk; St Kilda Road is well-served

## July Specifics
Arts Precinct is entirely indoor — perfect for a rainy Melbourne day or a cold July afternoon. The NGV's permanent collections are large enough to fill 2–3 hours. ACMI is more interactive and good for families or tech-minded visitors.

The walk from Fed Square across the Princes Bridge to Southbank is ~10 minutes and scenic even in rain.
