# Federation Square Research

## What It Is
Melbourne's civic gathering space and cultural precinct, opened 2002. Located directly opposite Flinders Street Station across the street. A sprawling plaza of contemporary architecture housing major cultural institutions. The site of major public events, screenings, and festivals.

## What's There
- **NGV (National Gallery of Victoria)** — two buildings: NGV International (Flinders Street side) and NGV Australia (Federation Square side)
- **ACMI (Australian Centre for the Moving Image)** — film, TV, and digital culture museum; free entry
- **Koorie Heritage Trust** — indigenous Australian culture and art
- **Ian Potter Centre** — Australian art (part of NGV)
- **Visitor Information Centre** — Melbourne and Victoria tourism info

## Practical Facts
- **Address:** Corner of Swanston Street & Flinders Street, Melbourne VIC 3000
- **Access:** Directly across from Flinders Street Station (no tram needed to reach it)
- **Hours:** Open air public space 24/7; individual institution hours vary
- **Cost:** Federation Square itself is free; NGV is free; ACMI is free

## Events
- Sports screenings on the big screen (AFL Grand Final, Olympic events)
- Melbourne International Arts Festival events
- NGV Friday Nights (seasonal)
- Live music and market stalls on weekends

## July Specifics
Federation Square is fully open-air but surrounded by NGV and ACMI which are fully enclosed. Good as a transit point between Flinders Street Station and Southbank. The NGV's winter exhibition program often runs October–March.
