# Melbourne Transport — Myki

Myki is Melbourne's integrated ticketing system for trains, trams, and buses. A reusable card with a chip — you top up with credit and touch on/off at validators.

## Key Facts
- **Card cost:** ~NT$134 refundable deposit at retail outlets, ~NT$224 at some machines
- **Top-up:** Online, at 7-Eleven, at station machines
- **Duration:** 2-hour (~NT$103), daily cap (~NT$206), weekly cap
- **Zones:** Zone 1 covers the CBD and inner suburbs (where you'll spend 95% of your time)

## The Free Tram Zone
Trams inside the "magic rectangle" (mostly CBD) are free — you don't need to touch on. Signs on tram stops indicate if you're in the zone.

## Train
The suburban rail network radiates from Flinders Street and Southern Cross stations. Use for trips beyond the inner suburbs (e.g., to [[st-kilda]] isn't by train — tram 96 is better).

## Tram
The tram network is extensive and iconic. Routes 96, 109, 12, 86 are useful for tourists. Trams are slower than walking for short CBD trips but essential for longer distances.
