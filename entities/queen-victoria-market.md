# Queen Victoria Market (QVM)

Melbourne's iconic produce market — a landmark since 1878. The northern terminus of Elizabeth Street, covering two city blocks. Open Tuesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday (closed Monday and Wednesday).

## Key Facts
- **Address:** Corner of Elizabeth Street & Victoria Street, Melbourne CBD
- **Hours:** Tue & Thu 6am–3pm, Fri 6am–5pm, Sat 6am–4pm, Sun 9am–4pm
- **Closed:** Monday, Wednesday
- **Myki:** Not needed

## What It Is

### The Market Hall
The main produce shed — fresh fruit, vegetables, meat, seafood, dairy, deli. This is where Melburnians shop. haggle is expected at some stalls.

### The Sheds
Covered sections with specialty traders — clothing, souvenirs, artisan goods.

### The Emu Bottom
The hill at the southern end — a large open-air car park on Tuesday/Thursday mornings.

### The Market at Night
Summer night markets (Wed evenings, Nov–Mar) — food stalls, bars, live music. Otherwise closes mid-afternoon.

## What It's Known For
- Cheap, excellent produce — way cheaper than supermarkets
- Souvenirs and Melbourne-themed gifts (cheaper than Fed Square)
- The Meat and Fish Hall
- The deli halls

## Tips
- Cash preferred by many stallholders (ATMs on site but expensive fees)
- Tuesday/Thursday mornings are the peak fresh produce days
- Avoid Sunday afternoon — it's winding down

## Connection to Concepts
- [[melbourne-cbd]] — at the northern edge of the CBD
- Melbourne's food and drink scene — covered in the market's own produce, deli, and takeaway options
