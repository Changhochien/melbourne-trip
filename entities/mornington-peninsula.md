# Mornington Peninsula

A peninsula south of Melbourne, jutting into Port Phillip Bay. Beach towns, wine regions, and geothermal hot springs. The flip side of Melbourne's urban density — calm, coastal, rural. Best experienced with a car.

## Key Areas

### Rye
The most practical base — central location, highest accommodation density, beach town. Best value for money. Close to hot springs (15 min drive) and wineries (20 min).

### Sorrento
Upmarket beachside town. The ferry to Queenscliff departs from here (Port Phillip Ferries). More expensive accommodation. Packed on summer weekends.

### Mount Martha
Upscale and quiet. Home to the Mornington Peninsula Hot Springs. Beach access. More residential.

### Portsea
At the very tip — near Point Nepean National Park. Luxury end of the market. Rocky beaches, coastal walks.

### Flinders
Small town on the western side of the peninsula. Wine region (Mornington Peninsula wine is pinot noir country). Quiet, scenic.

## Getting There
- **From Melbourne CBD:** M1 south ~1 hour to Rye
- **From Mount Martha hot springs:** ~15 min to Rye
- **Car essential** — public transport on the peninsula is very limited

## Key Activities

### Mornington Peninsula Hot Springs
- Located in Mount Martha
- Open-air geothermal pools built into a hillside
- Book online in advance — sells out, especially for evening sessions
- Plan for 3-4 hours
- Most accommodation packages in Rye include hot springs tickets

### Beaches
- Swimming beaches at Rosebud, Rye, Sorrento (Port Phillip side — calmer)
- Rockpool beaches at Portsea and Mount Martha (Southern Ocean side)
- Variety of coastal experiences in one peninsula

### Wineries
Mornington Peninsula is cool-climate pinot noir and chardonnay country. Cellar doors concentrated around Red Hill and Main Ridge. Notable producers:
- Ten Minutes by Tractor
- Port Phillip Estate
- Crittenden Estate

### Point Nepean National Park
At the peninsula's tip. Former military fortifications, coastal walks, historic Quarantine Station. Free entry, parking ~NT$112/day.
